Fish Curry

Desctiption

Dahi Machi is a healing dish of delicate white fish lightly fried and set in a velvety yogurt sauce. While fish and yogurt may not be the first thing on many American minds, don't knock it til you try it!

This recipe comes from a book given to me as a gift from a dear friend many years back. The original author of this blessed recipe is a Ms. Julie Sahni. I hope you enjoy it as much as I have.


Ingredients

  • 1lb skinless, boneless haddock fillets
  • 8tbsp light vegetable oil
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 cups finely chopped onions
  • 1 1/2tbsp finely chopped fresh ginger root
  • 1/8tsp turmeric
  • 3/4tsp Kosher salt
  • Small pinch each of ground cinnamon and clove
  • 3/4 cup plain yogurt
  • 2tbsp finely chopped fresh coriander leaves
  • 2-4 green chilies, seeded and minced

Steps

  1. Rinse with cold water and pat dry the haddock fillets then cut them into 1 inch by 2 inch pieces and set aside
  2. Heat 3 or 4 tablespoons of oil in a large non-stick pan over mid heat. Dust the fillets with flour, and add them to the pan in one layer. Fry until golden brown on all sides. Don't overcook. After having fried all fish pieces, set them to the side on a platter.
  3. Add remaining oil to the pan with onions. Fry until butterscotch in color, stirring frequently so as not to char or burn them. (Abt 15min) Add ginger, fry a minute, then add turmeric, cinnamon, salt, and clove, stirring rapidly for 15 seconds. Add yogurt and turn off heat.
  4. Pour onion mixture into a blender or food processor, and blend to a puree. Return sauce to pan and bring to a gentle simmer over low heat. Add the cooked fish pieces and simmer covered for an additional 3 minutes. Serve sprinkled with coriander leaves and chilies.
