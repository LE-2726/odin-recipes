Wisconsin Beer Brats

Description

Listen, Wisconsin is one of the most underrated states in the contiguous 48. Beer, squeaky cheese curds, the Packers, the Brewers, Kopps Custard, the list goes on. But nothing beats a brat fresh off the grill at Miller Park, and a crisp Miller Lite while the Brewers get their butt kicked! (Hey, it is what it is.)

I know not everybody has the benefit of living in or near Wisconsin, so I have found the best brat recipe to bring a bit of home to you. So crack open a cold one this football season, and cook up some Wisconsin brats. You deserve it!


Ingredients

  • 2lbs fresh bratwurst sausages
  • 6 (12 fluid oz) cans or bottles of beer
  • 2 onions, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup butter
  • 1 1/2tsp ground black pepper
  • 10 hoagie rolls

Steps

  1. Prick bratwurst with a fork to prevent them from exploding as they cook; place in a large stockpot. Add beer, onions, butter, and black pepper. Place the stockpot over medium heat, bring to a boil and simmer for 15 to 20 minutes.
  2. Preheat an outdoor grill for mid-high heat and lightly oil the grate.
  3. Cook bratwurst on the preheated grill until evenly browned, about 10 to 14 minutes, turning occasionally. Serve hot off the grill with onions on hoagie rolls.
